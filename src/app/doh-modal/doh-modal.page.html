DOH Guidelines


DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH
RETURN TO WORK
INTERIM GUIDELINES
Department Memorandum
as of May 11, 2020
Employers should increase the resilience of the employees/staff

Encourage the employees to eat a balanced diet and adopt healthy eating practices

Encourage employees to exercise regularly and provide opportunity for exercise

Institute activities that will cater to the mental health of the employees and staff. Allow for opportunity for virtual socialization, etc


Employers should put mechanisms to reduce transmission of COVID-19

Encourage the wearing of masks at all times

Impose regular handwashing and encourage proper hand hygiene

Remind employees and staff to practice cough etiquette and respiratory hygiene


Employers should put mechanisms to reduce contact and infeciousness

Ensure temperature and symptom monitoring of staff reporting for work

Establish referral network for employees who will develop symptoms

Enforce physical distancing

Visual reminders on infection control and prevention should be posted

Work place should be disinfected properly and regularly

Work place should have good ventilation


For workers/emloyees who fall under the vulnerable workforce, alternative workplace accomodations can be provided like:

Work from Home arrangements

Working in shifts to reduce number of employees present

Requiring the skeletal workforce only to report to work

Shelter near the workplace for employees


Screening of Returning Employees

Returning employees and workers physically reporting to their place of employment shall be screened and assessed for signs and symptoms of COVID19

  • sore throat
  • body pains
  • headache
  • fever
  • flu-like symptoms: cough, colds, and fatigue

The following exposures should also be assessed:

  • Face-to-face contact with a confirmed case within 1 meter and for more than 15 minutes
  • Direct physical contact with a confirmed case
  • Direct care for a patient with a probable/confirmed COVID-19 without using proper PPEs

A worker/emloyee stays or resides in an area where community transmission is still present

A worker /employee travelled from a country or area where community transmission is still present to an area without sustained community transmission

Actions to be taken for symptom screening:

  • With symptoms during the 14 days before Work Resumption?
  • With symptoms on the date of Work Resumption?

Not allowed to physically return to work and must consult primary care provider

Complete a 14-day Self Quarantine. Present a Certificateo of Quarantine Completion at their respective workplace after 14 days
